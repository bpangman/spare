10 Cost-Saving Ideas

Ideas for reducing operational costs and regulatory burden at launch.

–
Start with 1-3 direct nonprofit partnerships
Skip the directory/DAF entirely; sign direct agreements with BGCA or a few big charities, send via ACH. No Endaoment fees, no directory costs.
–
Be a referral, not a processor
Track round-ups, then send users a pre-filled donation link to Every.org or charity's own site. You never touch donation money → no solicitor registration, no DAF, no compliance overhead.
–
Use a fiscal sponsor
Operate under a 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor (e.g., Open Collective Foundation). They handle compliance + state registrations for 5-10% vs $15K+ in setup/filings.
–
Launch in exempt states only
Start in the 10 states with no registration requirement (AZ, DE, ID, IN, IA, MT, NE, SD, VT, WY); expand as revenue grows.
–
Go pure SaaS — no donation processing
Track round-ups; monthly "donate now" button opens the nonprofit's own donation page pre-filled. Charge $2-3/mo subscription. Zero compliance, zero DAF.
–
Use Every.org's free API as donation engine
If their enterprise pricing is reasonable, get directory + processing + tax receipts + compliance without custom build. Could cut costs 80%+.
–
Batch donations quarterly, not monthly
4 charges/year vs 12 per user. Saves ~$2.40/user/year in Stripe fees + reduces complexity.
–
Default to ACH, bury the card option
ACH costs ~$0.80/txn vs ~$1.03 card. Lower service fee (5% vs 10%) is also more attractive to users.
–
Launch as Chrome extension/widget first
Skip app stores and native builds. Cheaper to build and maintain while proving demand.
–
White-label an existing round-up app (e.g., Pledge.to)
Use their infrastructure under PocketCache brand. Focus on marketing; they handle plumbing.

Combo to explore first: Ideas #2 + #6 — use Every.org's API for the actual donations (501(c)(3) covers compliance), PocketCache only tracks round-ups and triggers donations. Cheapest path to launch.

How It Works — User Flow & Money
Onboarding (one time)

Blake downloads the app. After the welcome slides and account creation, he gets two setup screens back to back.

First: "Which card should we track?" He picks his Chase Visa. Plaid opens, he logs into Chase, done. PocketCache can now see every transaction on that card — read-only, can never touch it.

Second: "How should we collect your round-up payments?" He picks Bank Account (ACH). He connects his checking account via Stripe. He picks Boys & Girls Club as his cause and lands on the dashboard.

Every day after that

Plaid quietly watches his Chase Visa. He buys coffee for $4.30 → $0.70 round-up queued. Groceries $67.43 → $0.57 round-up queued. It all piles up silently. Blake sees nothing until the monthly charge.

End of month — the charge

Blake has accumulated $23.80 in round-ups. If it's under $5, it rolls to next month. Otherwise, PocketCache charges his checking account $23.80 via ACH. One clean charge on his bank statement, nothing on his Chase Visa.

Blake is charged (ACH)$23.80
Stripe ACH processing fee (0.8%)−$0.19
PocketCache platform fee (5% of $23.80)−$1.19
PocketCache net revenue (~$1.00 after Stripe)
Moves into Stripe Treasury$22.61
The float period

$22.61 sits in your Stripe Treasury account earning interest while you accumulate a full quarter of donations from all users. This is where PocketCache earns float income on top of platform fees.

End of quarter — disbursement

PocketCache sweeps the quarter's accumulated funds to Endaoment via their API. Say Blake donated ~$22/month — that's ~$67 for the quarter. Endaoment receives it and grants it directly to Boys & Girls Club. Endaoment emails Blake a tax receipt automatically. Their ~1.5% annual fee on the ~45-day hold comes to roughly $0.13 — negligible.

What Blake experienced

One monthly charge on his bank statement. One quarterly tax receipt. His Chase Visa was never charged. He never had to think about it.

Regulatory Checklist
💸 Money Transmission Licenses (MTL)
Solved
Stripe Treasury holds all required state MTLs. PocketCache is a platform customer, not a money transmitter. No licensing needed on our end.
📜 Charitable Solicitation Registration
Solved
Endaoment's DAF model handles solicitation registration across all ~41 states that require it. As the intermediary 501(c)(3), they're the one soliciting — not PocketCache.
🔒 PCI DSS (card data security)
Solved
Stripe Elements + Stripe Treasury keeps PocketCache at SAQ A compliance. Card numbers never touch our servers or code. No PCI audit required.
🏦 Consumer Consent for Bank Data (Plaid)
Solved
Plaid Link (the modal users interact with) handles all required CFPB consent disclosures inline. Users explicitly authorize access during onboarding.
📋 Terms of Service + Privacy Policy
Blocker
Required before Plaid will approve production access. Must disclose: what Plaid reads, how round-ups are calculated, fee structure (deducted from donation), how to cancel, data retention.
🧾 Tax Receipts for Donors
Solved
Endaoment issues IRS-compliant tax receipts automatically to users after each disbursement. PocketCache does not need to generate or manage these.
Tech Stack
📡
Plaid
Reads credit card transactions to calculate round-ups. Read-only, ~12,000 institutions. Cannot be replaced by Stripe for CC monitoring.
💳
Stripe (Payments + Elements + Financial Connections)
Collects monthly round-up payments via ACH, Apple Pay, or card. Stripe Elements handles card input (PCI-compliant). Financial Connections links bank accounts for ACH.
🏦
Stripe Treasury
Holds collected funds between monthly sweep and quarterly Endaoment disbursement. PocketCache earns interest on the float. Stripe holds all MTLs.
🏛
Endaoment
Registered 501(c)(3) donor-advised fund. Receives quarterly disbursements and grants to nonprofits. Handles charitable solicitation registration. Issues tax receipts automatically.

Backend Architecture

Full backend is stubbed in backend/ — real code structure, wired logic, TODOs where live credentials plug in. Not yet deployed; needs a server (Railway, Render, Fly.io, etc.).

📅
Daily job — 2am every night
daily-roundups.js — Fetches new Plaid transactions, calculates round-ups, saves to DB. Filters own charges by amount + date + last4 to prevent infinite loop.
📅
Monthly job — 1st of month, 6am
monthly-charge.js — Sums round-ups per user. Charges via Stripe if ≥$5. Deducts platform fee (5% ACH, 10% card/Apple Pay). Deposits net to Stripe Treasury. Retry once after 3 days on failure; pause + notify on second failure.
📅
Quarterly job — Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1 at 8am
quarterly-sweep.js — Gets Treasury balance, initiates ACH OutboundTransfer to Endaoment bank, submits grants via Endaoment API proportionally per nonprofit. Endaoment sends tax receipts to users.
🔁
Stripe webhook handler
webhooks.js — Listens for payment_intent.succeeded, payment_intent.payment_failed, treasury.outbound_transfer.posted. Confirms charges and disbursements asynchronously.
🗄
Database schema (SQLite)
schema.sql — Tables: users, plaid_connections, payment_methods, roundups, monthly_charges, treasury_log, quarterly_disbursements.
🔌
API routes
POST /api/plaid/link-token — creates Plaid Link token for frontend
POST /api/plaid/exchange — exchanges public token for access token
POST /api/stripe/setup-intent — creates SetupIntent for Stripe Elements
POST /api/stripe/financial-session — creates FC session for ACH
POST /api/stripe/save-payment-method — attaches pm to customer
